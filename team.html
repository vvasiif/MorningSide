Julianna Randé

Julianna unexpectedly entered into the world of Montessori in 2009. She immediately fell in love with the method and has embraced her world in this philosophy for the past 13 years. She holds a Master of Fine Arts in ... English Philology from the Jagiellonian University. She has a postgraduate degree in Psychological and Pedagogical Counselling and Support. She also has a Montessori Lower Elementary and Upper Elementary diploma. She is currently finishing her School Leadership Montessori diploma from the USA. Julianna is originally from Hungary and Poland, however, she spent the majority of her childhood in London. She has lived in a number of countries, including Italy and Spain. She loves to travel and to learn about new cultures. Julianna’s true vocation is being a guide and inspiring others to see the beauty and magic of the Montessori philosophy. She loves children and helping them to grow into individuals. She deeply loves the Montessori approach and can’t imagine her life any other way. In her free time she enjoys taking long walks, dancing and relaxing through yoga having completed a Yoga Teacher Training Course in Thailand.

Briana Kay Kyle

Briana has been a guide in the Morningside 6-9 community since the autumn of 2019. Briana grew up on a farm in Whitewater, Wisconsin. Her parents’ farm had cows, sheep, goats, rabbits, ducks, geese and chickens. This ... experience cultivated a deep love of the natural world. She enjoys art, camping, hiking, kayaking, reading and photography. After high school, she attended the University of Wisconsin-Madison receiving a BSc in Biological Aspects of Conservation. Following graduation she began working with students at Nature’s Classroom Institute and Montessori School. She began as an environmental educator leading groups of students aged 6-14 in exploration of the natural world. Through this position she fell in love with the Montessori philosophy of education. As a result, she spent the next two years as an assistant in the elementary program at Nature’s Classroom Montessori. In the summer of 2016, she attended Seton Montessori Institute where she received her AMS Montessori 6-12 Elementary Credential, then spent three years as the lead guide at Nature’s Classroom Montessori prior to coming to Cyprus in the autumn of 2019. She loves learning and growing alongside the children as they explore and discover the world. She is an avid supporter of Montessori Education, viewing each child as an individual with unique interests, talents, gifts, strengths and potential. Through respect and understanding she will strive to help each of your children become independent, self-aware, self-disciplined and motivated learners, and global citizens.
